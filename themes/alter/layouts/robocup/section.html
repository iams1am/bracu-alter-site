What is RoboCup Rescue?

The RoboCup Rescue Robot League is one of the most prestigious robotics competitions in the world. Often referred to as the "World Cup of Robotics," it brings together university teams from across the globe to push the boundaries of autonomous robot capabilities in disaster response scenarios.

Robots must navigate complex, unstructured environments that simulate the aftermath of earthquakes, building collapses, and industrial accidents. Teams are evaluated on their ability to perform autonomous mapping, victim identification, hazardous material detection, and real-time data relay to human operators — all under strict time constraints.

20+ Years Running
30+ Countries
100+ Teams Worldwide

Who Competes?

Teams from top research universities and institutions worldwide participate, including groups from Japan, Germany, USA, China, Iran, Thailand, and many others. Each team designs, builds, and programs fully custom robots specifically for disaster response — testing autonomy, mechanical resilience, perception, and operator tools under real competition pressure.

BRACU ALTER at RoboCup

BRACU ALTER first appeared at RoboCup Rescue in 2024, reaching the finals in Eindhoven, Netherlands — a remarkable debut for a team from Bangladesh. In 2025, we returned as the sole team representing Bangladesh at the World Championship in Salvador, Brazil, further cementing our position on the global robotics stage.

Each year we iterate on our rover design, improve our autonomy stack, and train our operators to perform under the intense pressure of competition day. Explore our campaigns below.
