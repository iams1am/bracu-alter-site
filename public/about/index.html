About Us

BRACU ALTER is a student-led research team from the Laboratory of Space System Engineering & Technology (LaSSET) within the BSRM School of Engineering at BRAC University.

We develop advanced, modular rescue rovers and autonomous guided vehicles designed for surveillance, inspection, and high-risk search and rescue operations. Our platforms are engineered for power efficiency and reliable navigation in rough, disaster-prone terrain — supporting faster and safer response when every second matters.

By integrating cutting-edge robotics and artificial intelligence, ALTER is committed to improving the speed, safety, and efficiency of rescue operations during earthquakes, floods, and industrial accidents. The team blends academic research with real-world application, striving to empower first responders and save lives.

RoboCup Rescue

World Championship 2025 Finalist 2024 Salvador, Brazil Eindhoven, Netherlands

The RoboCup Rescue Robot League is a global arena where the brightest minds converge to push the boundaries of robotics and artificial intelligence. Teams design autonomous robots to aid emergency responders in hazardous environments.

In 2025, BRACU ALTER represented Bangladesh as the sole team at the World Championship in Salvador, Brazil. The year before, the team reached the finals of RoboCup Rescue 2024 in Eindhoven, Netherlands — marking a significant milestone for BRAC University and Bangladesh's robotics scene.

National Achievements

IDEA 5.0 Champion VC's Special Recognition Hult Prize 1st Runner-Up

Crowned champions of IDEA Innovation 5.0 — one of Bangladesh's most prestigious national tech competitions — standing out among over 200 university teams.

The team was awarded the VC Sir's Special Recognition Award in Fall 2024 for outstanding contributions to engineering and robotics at BRAC University. BRACU ALTER also secured 1st Runner-Up at the campus-level Hult Prize competition.
